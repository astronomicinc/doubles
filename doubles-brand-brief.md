# doubles Brand Brief

**Brand Name:** doubles  
**Domain:** doubles.singles  
**Tagline:** "Strategy. Partnership. Connection."

---

## 1. POSITIONING & CONTEXT

**Core Positioning:**
Exclusive dating events for post-exit founders, high-net-worth executives ($200K+/year), and investors in San Francisco. We host intimate (30-person) events at private founder homes where every attendee brings a vetted single friend of the opposite sex, ensuring gender balance, quality, and genuine intention to connect.

**Target Audience:**
- Post-exit founders (sold a company, or leading $50M+ ARR startup)
- High-earning executives (VPs at major tech companies, profitable business founders)
- Angel investors, VCs, advisors
- Age range: 30–50 (median ~40)
- Location: San Francisco Bay Area only
- Income: $200K+/year or $1M+ net worth
- Mindset: Ambitious, quality-focused, dating-app fatigued, values community over convenience

**Why It Exists:**
Successful people have few ways to meet potential partners without compromising on quality, time, or discretion. Dating apps fail this audience (algorithmic, flakes, transactional). Traditional singles events feel desperate (100+ people, mixed quality). We're the alternative: a curated community where the people matter as much as the concept. Human vetting (your friend vouches for you) beats algorithmic matching every time.

## 2. VISUAL AESTHETIC & DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**Overall Aesthetic: Luxury Minimalism**

Think **Raya** or **Soho House**—sophisticated, understated, photography-driven. We are NOT playful, NOT gimmicky, NOT web2.0. We are elegant, intentional, and refined.

**Color Palette:**
- **Primary:** Charcoal (#2C2C2C) — authority, sophistication
- **Secondary:** Cream (#F5F1E8) — warmth, luxury
- **Tertiary:** White (#FFFFFF) — clean, minimal
- **Accent (optional):** Slate (#6B7280) or soft gold (#D4A574) — subtle sophistication, use sparingly
- **Text:** Charcoal on cream/white, or white on charcoal. Never more than 2 colors in any view.
- **No bright colors.** No tennis colors. No playful pastels.

**Typography:**
- **Headlines:** Refined serif (Garamond, Sabon, or similar) or modern serif (Freight Text, Lyon Text)
  - Alternative: Clean sans-serif (Calibre, Helvetica Neue, Inter) if serif feels dated
  - Size hierarchy: H1 (48–64px), H2 (32–40px), H3 (24–28px), Body (16–18px)
  - Generous line-height (1.6–1.8) for elegance and readability
- **Body copy:** Refined serif or clean sans-serif, 16–18px, dark gray/charcoal
- **UI text (buttons, labels):** Clean sans-serif, 14–16px, medium weight
- **Avoid:** Anything trendy (thin ultra-light fonts, decorative fonts). Go timeless.

**Photography & Imagery:**
- **Hero images:** Agi House bridge views (Golden Gate, Bay Bridge visible), Portola House elegant interior
- **Venue photography:** High-resolution, professionally lit or naturally lit during golden hour
- **Attendee photos:** Candid moments (people talking, laughing, toasting), NOT posed group shots or couples looking at camera
- **Minimalism in imagery:** Let photos breathe. Not every page needs photography. Sometimes whitespace is more powerful.
- **Photo treatment:** Subtle vignetting or soft edges okay. NO filters, NO color overlays, NO watermarks.

**Layout & Spacing:**
- **Grid:** 12-column grid, generous gutters (32–48px on desktop)
- **Breathing room:** Whitespace is luxury. Avoid cramped layouts.
- **Sections:** Clear visual separation (whitespace, subtle dividers, color blocks)
- **Buttons:** Minimal, elegant. Subtle shadow or border on hover. NO neon colors, NO oversized CTAs.
- **Responsive:** Mobile-first. Tablet and desktop scale proportionally.

## 3. BRAND PERSONALITY & TONE OF VOICE

**Personality Traits:**
- Sophisticated (not stuffy)
- Intentional (not casual)
- Confident (not arrogant)
- Slightly self-aware (acknowledges dating awkwardness without being cheesy)
- Respectful of audience intelligence (no "find your soulmate" language)
- Community-focused (not transactional)

**Tone in Different Contexts:**

**Positioning/Value Prop:**
"We host invitation-only dating events in the private homes of SF's most ambitious founders and executives. Every event is intimate (30 people), every attendee is vetted by someone you trust, and every venue has a story."

**Bring-a-Friend Mechanic:**
"Everyone here was brought by someone you'd want in your network. That's better than any algorithm. The person vouching for them is also single and vouching. Their reputation is on the line."

**Exclusivity/Approval:**
"We're selective. Not because we're snobby, but because quality matters. We approve based on fit, not just ability to pay."

**Event Description (Example):**
"The next Deuce is at [Founder]'s home in [Neighborhood]. Twin Peaks views, cocktails, and 30 people you'd actually want to meet. Bring your strongest single friend."

**Success Narrative:**
"Game, set, match." (When someone finds a connection)

**Language to Use:**
- Tennis references (court, deuce, game, serve, volley, match, double down, on the doubles)
- "Vetted," "curated," "intentional," "quality," "serious," "community"
- "Strategy," "partnership," "connection"
- Active voice, clear instructions

**Language to Avoid:**
- Generic dating language ("find your soulmate," "the one," "your perfect match")
- Overly formal/corporate language
- "Networking" or "business" framing
- Crass wealth references ("accredited investor dating," "millionaire matchmaking")
- Emoji or cutesy visual language
- Hype language or urgency language ("limited spots," "last chance")

## 6. DESIGN SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

Claude Design should create a **Design System** document that includes:

1. **Color System**
   - Primary, secondary, tertiary, accent colors
   - Text colors (body, headings, UI)
   - Hover/active states
   - Do NOT create a colorful palette; stick to the minimal palette above

2. **Typography System**
   - Font families (choose serif OR modern sans, not both)
   - Font scales (H1–H6, Body, Caption, UI)
   - Line heights, letter spacing
   - Font weights (regular, medium, bold)

3. **Spacing System**
   - Base unit (8px or 4px grid)
   - Scales (8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96px)
   - Margins, padding, gaps

4. **Component Library**
   - Buttons (primary, secondary, tertiary, disabled, hover, active states)
   - Input fields (text, email, select, textarea, focus, error states)
   - Cards (testimonial card, venue card, event card)
   - Navigation (header, footer, breadcrumbs)
   - Forms (application form layout, field grouping)
   - Modal/Dialog (approval, rejection, confirmation)
   - States (default, hover, active, disabled, loading, error, success)

5. **Layout Patterns**
   - Hero section
   - Two-column layout (image + text)
   - Three-column grid (venues, testimonials)
   - Full-width section (value props, CTAs)
   - Responsive breakpoints (mobile: 375px, tablet: 768px, desktop: 1280px)

6. **Interactive Patterns**
   - Button interactions (hover, active, disabled)
   - Form validation (real-time, on-blur, on-submit)
   - Navigation (sticky/fixed, mobile hamburger)
   - Scroll behavior (parallax on hero, fade-in on scroll, optional)

## 7. BRAND VOICE EXAMPLES

**Homepage Hero:**
"Bring your strongest single friend. Find your match."

"Exclusive dating events for post-exit founders and high-net-worth singles in SF. Every attendee brings a vetted friend of the opposite sex, ensuring gender balance, quality, and genuine intention to connect."
