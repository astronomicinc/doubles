Event Code of Conduct

Guidelines for creating an intentional, respectful community.

Our Commitment

Doubles events are built on a simple premise: good people, in a carefully curated space, with clear intentions. This code of conduct ensures that every attendee—whether they find a match or a new collaborator—leaves with the confidence that they were safe, respected, and in a room full of serious people.

Core Principles

Treat People How You'd Want to Be Treated

You're here because someone you trust vouched for you. Extend that same respect to others. Approach conversations with genuine curiosity, not as a transaction.

Consent and Boundaries Matter

Respect people's verbal and non-verbal cues. If someone isn't interested in continuing a conversation, gracefully move on. No means no. Hesitation means no. Boundaries are not rejections—they're clarity.

No Harassment or Discrimination

We have zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or unwanted behavior based on gender, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, age, or any other characteristic. This includes inappropriate touching, unsolicited comments, or persistent pursuit after someone has declined interest.

Be Authentic

Don't misrepresent yourself in your application or at the event. Honesty about your situation, intentions, and who you are is what makes this work. If you're recently out of a relationship, or unsure about what you want, that's fine—just be honest about it.

Respect Privacy

What happens at the event stays at the event. Don't share photos of attendees without permission. Don't gossip or share personal details about others. Protect the privacy of the community.

What Happens If You Violate the Code

We take safety and respect seriously. Violations may result in:

  • A conversation with our team about what happened
  • Removal from the current event
  • A ban from future events

In cases of serious misconduct (harassment, assault, threats), we will not hesitate to involve authorities or take legal action.

If You Witness or Experience a Violation

If you witness behavior that violates this code, or if you experience harassment or discomfort, please speak with an event organizer immediately. We're here to make sure everyone feels safe.

You can also email chris@astronomic.com to report concerns confidentially.

Questions?

Reach out. We're building a community, and that means being responsive to feedback and concerns. Email us with questions about this code.
