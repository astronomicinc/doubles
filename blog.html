Thoughts on dating, connection, and community.

May 22, 2026 5 min read

Why Dating Apps Failed Founders

If you've built a company, you understand vetting. You know the difference between a resume and a person. Yet dating apps ask us to ignore all that wisdom.

There's a paradox at the heart of dating apps: they were built to optimize for engagement, not connection. Every swipe, every match, every message is a data point in a machine learning model designed to keep you swiping longer, not to actually help you meet someone real.

As a founder, you've internalized the opposite. You know that the best hires never come from algorithms. They come from someone you trust saying, "You need to meet this person." That introduction carries weight. Someone's reputation is on the line.

When was the last time a dating app introduced you to someone like that? Not a profile. A real introduction from someone who knows both of you and thinks you're worth each other's time?

That's what we're building with Doubles. Not another algorithm. Just the oldest and most reliable vetting mechanism humans have: a friend who knows you both.

May 20, 2026 6 min read

Building Community for the Ambitious

The best networks in tech weren't built on apps. They were built in homes, over drinks, between people who got each other.

Venture capital firms don't introduce startups to investors through an algorithm. They host dinners. They bring people together in rooms where something might happen. Why? Because the algorithm was never the mechanism of connection—people are.

We started Doubles because we noticed that while founder networks thrive (thanks to conferences, accelerators, WhatsApp groups), dating remains stuck in an algorithmic dark age. A space built for friction and swiping, not for the kind of intentional, high-stakes introductions that actually work.

What if dating events for successful people worked the same way investor dinners do? Intimate. Curated. Built on the premise that when you're selective about who you spend time with professionally, you'd be just as selective about who you spend time with romantically.

That's the insight behind Doubles. We're not inventing something new. We're scaling something old: the power of a good friend saying, "I know someone you should meet." And doing it in the homes of people who've already built something remarkable.

May 18, 2026 4 min read

The Future of Dating in Tech

In five years, we believe the best way for ambitious people to meet will look nothing like today's apps.

Dating apps optimized for speed. They showed you as many people as possible in as little time as possible. Swipe, swipe, swipe. It was efficient, but efficiency was the problem. Connection isn't efficient. It's selective. It's slow. It's about choosing carefully, not maximizing options.

The next generation of dating will move backwards—not in sophistication, but in philosophy. It will be more like how founders meet each other: through trusted introductions, in spaces that feel intentional, with people who were vetted by someone you already respect.

That's our vision for Doubles. We're starting in San Francisco with founders and investors. But the model scales: Austin. New York. London. Singapore. Anywhere there's a density of ambitious people who want to meet partners the way they meet collaborators—with intention, with vetting, with people they trust bringing them together.

We're not trying to replace dating apps. We're building the alternative they failed to be: a system designed for connection, not engagement.
