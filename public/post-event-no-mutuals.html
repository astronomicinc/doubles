Things this doesn't mean.

It doesn't mean nobody was thinking about you. It doesn't mean Sarah, James, or anyone you marked saw your pick and passed. They never saw your picks. Your one-way picks stay private forever — that's the contract.

It doesn't mean nobody at all marked you. Some people in the room might have picked you and we won't tell either of you, because the other side wasn't mutual. That's the whole point of mutual-only. Both sides have to show up before we say anything.

And it doesn't mean Vol. 01 was a wash. The room you sat in was the room. The conversations that happened, happened. A mutual is a bonus, not the goal.

A small honest thing

Across the founding volume, about half of attendees come away with at least one mutual. Half don't. We've been there, both ways — and the half without mutuals usually leave with something else: a new friend they wouldn't have met, a job lead, a question worth chasing.

What now.

The Tuesday window is closed for Vol. 01. If you'd like a professional intro to someone you met, we can still help — reply to your acceptance email with a name and a one-line "why" and we'll reach out as a normal warm intro, the old-fashioned way.

For Vol. 02 — you're auto-rolled into the next pool. No re-apply. The room composition shifts every quarter; people you'd click with might be in September's room and not have been in this one.
