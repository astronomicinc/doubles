# doubles Design Inspiration & Reference

This document provides aesthetic references, competitor analysis, and inspiration for Claude Design to use when creating mockups and design system.

---

## DESIGN AESTHETIC REFERENCES

### Primary Inspiration: Luxury Community Brands

**1. Raya (Dating App)**
- **Why:** Premium positioning, selective, elegant design
- **Aesthetic Notes:**
  - Minimal color palette (black, white, gold accents)
  - Typography: serif headlines, sans-serif body
  - Photography-first design (not people, but settings/vibes)
  - Lots of whitespace
  - Exclusivity signaled through design, not copy
- **What to Borrow:** Minimalism, photography focus, selective color palette
- **What NOT to Borrow:** Dating app language, app-like interactions, heavy text

**2. Soho House (Private Club)**
- **Why:** Community positioning, luxury without snobbishness, membership model
- **Aesthetic Notes:**
  - Charcoal, cream, gold palette (matches our brief)
  - Generous whitespace
  - High-quality photography of spaces/community
  - Serif typography (sophisticated)
  - Emphasis on "members" (community language)
  - Mixed media: text, photography, video
- **What to Borrow:** Color palette, whitespace, photography, community framing
- **What NOT to Borrow:** Over-designed/ornate elements, too much gold/luxury visual metaphor

**3. The Well (Private Community)**
- **Why:** Positioning as "infrastructure for community," not transactional
- **Aesthetic Notes:**
  - Minimal design
  - Emphasis on people and connection
  - Quality typography
  - Warm (cream/off-white) backgrounds
- **What to Borrow:** Community-first framing, warmth, minimalism
- **What NOT to Borrow:** Corporate feel (it's slightly more formal than we want)

**4. Maven (Matchmaking Service)**
- **Why:** Premium positioning, quality-focused, not desperate-feeling
- **Aesthetic Notes:**
  - Clean, minimal
  - High-end photography of spaces
  - Serif typography
  - Emphasis on expertise and curation
- **What to Borrow:** Curation language, photography focus, minimal design
- **What NOT to Borrow:** One-on-one matchmaker framing (we're community, not concierge)

## VISUAL STYLE BREAKDOWN

### Color Palette Analysis
**Raya:** Black (#000) + white + gold (#D4AF37) accents  
**Soho House:** Charcoal (#2C2C2C) + cream (#F5F1E8) + gold accents  
**The Well:** Off-white (#FAF9F6) + dark gray + minimal color  
**Maven:** White + charcoal + minimal accent  

**Our Palette (recommended):**
- Charcoal #2C2C2C (authority)
- Cream #F5F1E8 (warmth, luxury)
- White #FFFFFF (clean, minimal)
- Optional accent: Slate #6B7280 or subtle gold #D4A574

### Typography Preferences
**Raya:** Serif headlines + sans-serif body  
**Soho House:** Serif throughout (classic)  
**The Well:** Modern sans-serif (clean)  
**Maven:** Serif headlines + sans-serif body  

**Our Recommendation:** 
- **Option A:** Refined serif (Garamond, Sabon) for headlines + serif for body (classic luxury)
- **Option B:** Serif headlines + clean sans-serif body (modern luxury)
- **Avoid:** Thin fonts, trendy display fonts, decorative fonts

### Photography Style
**Raya:** Settings/vibes, minimalist, moody, some people but mostly environments  
**Soho House:** Spaces, community moments, high production value  
**The Well:** People + spaces, candid moments, warm feeling  
**Maven:** Spaces, professional photography  

**Our Reference:**
- Primary: Venue photography (Agi House, Portola House)
- Secondary: Candid event moments (people talking, toasting, laughing)
- Avoid: Posed shots, cheesy couple imagery, stock photos
- Treatment: Natural light preferred, subtle vignette okay, NO filters, NO overlays, NO watermarks

## WHAT NOT TO DO (Inspiration from What Fails)

### Competitor Examples to Avoid Aesthetic

**The Meet Group / Match.com**
- **Problem:** Too corporate, too "dating startup," generic
- **Visual:** Loud colors, many icons, oversaturated photography
- **Tone:** Pushy urgency, "find love now"
- **Why we avoid it:** Feels desperate, not exclusive

**Bumble / Hinge (Generic Dating App Aesthetic)**
- **Problem:** App-like, swipe culture, millennial pink/bright colors
- **Visual:** Lots of color, rounded corners, illustrations
- **Tone:** "Have fun! Find love! It's casual!"
- **Why we avoid it:** We're NOT a dating app. We're a community.

**Luxury Real Estate / Realtor Sites**
- **Problem:** Over-designed, too much imagery, corporate photos
- **Visual:** Huge photos, lots of flourishes, gold/marble aesthetic
- **Tone:** "Buy this house! It's amazing!"
- **Why we avoid it:** We're not selling real estate. Homes are the venue, not the product.

**Generic Event Sites (Eventbrite, Meetup)**
- **Problem:** Too many sections, call-outs, social proof boxes
- **Visual:** Busy, cramped, lots of colors and buttons
- **Tone:** "Join now! Limited spots!"
- **Why we avoid it:** Feels transactional, not exclusive

## SPECIFIC DESIGN DIRECTION

### Hero Section Style
**Inspiration:** Raya, Soho House, The Well  
**What works:**
- Full-width hero with hero image (Agi House bridge views)
- Hero text overlaid or beside image (white text on dark overlay, or text on charcoal background beside image)
- Minimal text (headline + subheading + CTA)
- No animations or over-design
- CTA should be obvious but understated (charcoal button, white text)

**What doesn't work:**
- Carousel/slider (too busy)
- Multiple CTAs (just one primary)
- Too much copy in hero
- Bright colors or gradients

### Testimonial Section Style
**Inspiration:** Soho House, Raya  
**What works:**
- Card layout (3 cards per row on desktop)
- Quote text large, name/role smaller
- Optional photo above or beside quote
- White/cream card background
- Subtle shadow or border
- Emphasis on VENUE in quote ("I went on a date at...")

**What doesn't work:**
- Star ratings (feels generic)
- Fake avatars or icons
- Multiple colors per card
- Quotes too long (keep to 2–3 sentences)

### Form/Application Section Style
**Inspiration:** Raya (application-based), Soho House (membership)  
**What works:**
- Single column form on mobile, maybe 2 columns on desktop
- Clear labels and helper text
- Large, readable font
- Whitespace around form
- Single submit button
- Confirmation message after submit

**What doesn't work:**
- Multi-step forms (keep it simple)
- Lots of fields (only ask what you need)
- Inline validation (show on blur or submit)
- Multiple CTAs or distracting elements near form

### CTA Button Style
**Inspiration:** Soho House, The Well, Raya  
**What works:**
- Charcoal background, white text
- Generous padding (14–16px vertical, 28–32px horizontal)
- No hard shadows (subtle shadow okay)
- Hover state: slightly darker or slightly lighter (subtle change)
- Readable, medium-weight font
- All caps or title case (not all lowercase)

**What doesn't work:**
- Bright colors (no neons, no contrasting colors)
- Rounded corners (keep subtle or square)
- Animations on hover (simple opacity/color change okay)
- Multiple button sizes/styles (keep consistent)

## COLOR PSYCHOLOGY & BRAND ASSOCIATIONS

**Charcoal (#2C2C2C):**
- Signals: Authority, sophistication, stability, seriousness
- Good for: Headlines, primary brand color, trust
- Avoid: Using only charcoal (too serious)

**Cream (#F5F1E8):**
- Signals: Warmth, luxury, nostalgia, comfort
- Good for: Backgrounds, approachability, softness
- Avoid: Using too much (can feel dated)

**White (#FFFFFF):**
- Signals: Cleanliness, minimalism, luxury, emptiness
- Good for: Whitespace, backgrounds, breathing room
- Avoid: Using plain white on white (no contrast)

**Slate Gray (#6B7280):**
- Signals: Neutrality, professionalism, subtlety
- Good for: Secondary text, dividers, accents
- Avoid: Using as primary text color (too light)

**Optional Gold (#D4A574 or similar):**
- Signals: Luxury, exclusivity, prestige
- Good for: Very subtle accents, luxury callouts
- Avoid: Using too much (feels dated or tacky)

### Serif Font Recommendations
- **Garamond** (classic, timeless, elegant)
- **Sabon** (refined, literary, sophisticated)
- **Freight Text** (modern serif, contemporary feel)
- **Lyon Text** (high-end, premium feel)

### Sans-Serif Font Recommendations
- **Inter** (clean, modern, highly readable)
- **Calibre** (geometric, contemporary, premium feel)
- **Helvetica Neue** (classic, timeless, versatile)
- **Maax** (modern, minimalist)

### Font Pairing Examples
- **Option 1:** Garamond (serif headlines) + Inter (sans-serif body)
- **Option 2:** Sabon (serif headlines) + Calibre (sans-serif UI)
- **Option 3:** Lyon Text (serif throughout, different weights for hierarchy)
- **Avoid:** More than 2 fonts. Avoid trendy fonts.

## SPACING & SCALE GUIDANCE

### Spacing Scale
- **Base:** 8px or 4px grid
- **Common scales:** 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96px
- **Whitespace:** Don't be afraid of it. Use 48–96px margins between major sections.
- **Padding:** 24–32px for card padding, 16–24px for button padding

### Font Size Scale
- **H1:** 48–64px (hero headlines)
- **H2:** 32–40px (section headers)
- **H3:** 24–28px (subheaders)
- **Body:** 16–18px (main text)
- **Caption:** 12–14px (labels, fine print)
- **Mobile:** Reduce by 10–20%, but maintain hierarchy

### Line Height Recommendations
- **Headlines:** 1.2–1.3 (tight, prestigious feel)
- **Body:** 1.6–1.8 (generous, elegant feel)
- **UI text:** 1.4–1.5 (clear, scannable)

## ANIMATION & INTERACTION GUIDANCE

**What's appropriate for doubles:**
- Subtle fade-in on page load (on hero text, optional)
- Smooth scroll behavior (natural, not janky)
- Button hover state (color change or slight opacity shift)
- Form field focus state (border color change, not dramatic)
- Modal open/close (fade-in/out, not dramatic bounce)

**What's NOT appropriate:**
- Page transitions (too trendy, distracting)
- Parallax scrolling (feels gimmicky)
- Lots of animations (kills sophistication)
- Micro-animations everywhere (too busy)
- Anything that feels "playful" or "fun" (not our brand)

**General Rule:** Motion should be subtle (200–400ms duration) and serve a purpose (indicate interactivity, guide focus). If you wonder if an animation is too much, it probably is.

## RESPONSIVE DESIGN PRIORITIES

**Mobile-First Approach:**
1. Design for 375px width first
2. Expand to tablet (768px) and desktop (1280px)
3. Touch targets should be 44px minimum height
4. Font sizes should be readable (16px minimum for inputs on iOS to avoid zoom)
5. Images full-width with aspect ratio maintained
6. Buttons full-width or very large on mobile

**Breakpoints:**
- Mobile: 375–767px
- Tablet: 768–1023px
- Desktop: 1024px+

**Responsive Changes:**
- 2–3 column layouts collapse to 1 column on mobile
- Hero section text may shift from overlay to full-width below image
- Testimonial cards: 3 columns desktop → 1 column mobile
- Form: maybe 2 columns on desktop, 1 on mobile

## FINAL CHECKLIST FOR CLAUDE DESIGN

When creating mockups and design system, verify:

- [ ] Color palette: Charcoal, cream, white (+ optional subtle accent)
- [ ] Typography: Max 2 fonts (serif + sans or serif only)
- [ ] Spacing: Generous margins between sections, no cramped layouts
- [ ] Venue photography: Prominent, high-quality, full-width hero
- [ ] Testimonials: Feature venue in quote, candid photography
- [ ] CTAs: Clear, obvious, charcoal button with white text
- [ ] Mobile-first: Responsive, touch-friendly, readable
- [ ] Tone: Sophisticated, not playful; community-driven, not transactional
- [ ] No icons, no emojis, no playful graphics
- [ ] No tennis rackets/courts/balls in design
- [ ] No bright colors, no gradients, no filters on photos
- [ ] Whitespace prioritized over content density
- [ ] Animation: Subtle or none (not playful)
- [ ] Accessibility: High contrast text, readable fonts, keyboard navigation

## DESIGN SYSTEM SCOPE

Claude Design should create a comprehensive Design System document including:

1. **Color Tokens** (named colors with hex codes)
2. **Typography Tokens** (font families, scales, weights)
3. **Spacing Tokens** (margin/padding scales)
4. **Component Library** (buttons, forms, cards, navigation, testimonials, etc.)
5. **Layout Patterns** (hero, 2-column, 3-column grid, full-width, etc.)
6. **State Patterns** (hover, active, disabled, loading, error, success)
7. **Responsive Breakpoints** (mobile, tablet, desktop)
8. **Interaction Patterns** (form validation, button states, modal behavior)
9. **Accessibility Notes** (color contrast, focus states, readability)

This design system becomes the source of truth for all website development.

## SUCCESS METRICS

When Claude Design is done, the design should:
- ✓ Feel timeless, not trendy (no skeuomorphism, no flat design fads)
- ✓ Evoke luxury without being ostentatious
- ✓ Emphasize photography and whitespace over graphics
- ✓ Communicate exclusivity and quality through design, not copy
- ✓ Be fully responsive without losing elegance on mobile
- ✓ Follow the charcoal/cream/white palette faithfully
- ✓ Have clear visual hierarchy (headlines, body, captions, UI text)
- ✓ Feature venue and community imagery prominently
- ✓ Make CTAs obvious without being pushy
- ✓ Feel like a "community" not a "startup" or "dating app"
