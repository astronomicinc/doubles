# doubles Website Structure & Content

**Website Goal:** Convert PEF members and high-net-worth SF singles into event attendees. Build credibility, explain the model, lower friction for application.

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- Application completion rate (target: 40%+ of visitors who reach the form)
- Approval rate (target: 50–60% of applications)
- Repeat attendance (target: 30%+ of Month 1–2 attendees attend Month 3+ events)

---

## SITE ARCHITECTURE

**7 Core Pages:**
1. Home (hero + value prop)
2. How It Works (bring-a-friend mechanic)
3. Venues (Agi House, Portola House, future partners)
4. Testimonials (social proof)
5. FAQ (objection handling)
6. Application (Luma embed)
7. About (brief founder story, optional)

**Navigation:**
- Top navigation bar: Logo (home) | How It Works | Venues | Testimonials | FAQ | Apply
- Mobile: Hamburger menu, same items
- Footer: Logo | Links | Email signup (optional) | Social (optional, minimal)

## PAGE 5: FAQ (Objection Handling)

**URL:** /faq  
**Goal:** Remove objections. Answer questions about pricing, format, vetting, expectations.

### FAQ Questions & Answers

**Q: What is doubles?**
A: "Exclusive dating events for post-exit founders, executives, and investors in SF. Every attendee brings a vetted single friend of the opposite sex. 30 people per event. Gender-balanced. Intentional. Real connections."

**Q: Who should attend?**
A: "Post-exit founders. VPs at major tech companies. Profitable business owners. Angel investors. Anyone earning $200K+/year or with $1M+ net worth. Anyone who's tired of dating apps and wants quality over convenience."

**Q: How much does it cost?**
A: "$110 per person per event. No membership required. You pay for you and your guest (so $220 per pair). Catering and venue are included."

**Q: What's the bring-a-friend thing about?**
A: "You bring someone single. They bring someone single. It ensures gender balance (15 men, 15 women). It also ensures vetting. You vouch for your friend's character. They vouch for yours. No catfishing. No flakes."

**Q: What if I don't know anyone single to bring?**
A: "No problem. Reach out in your application. We can suggest compatible people from our community, or help you find someone. You don't have to come alone, though—that's the point of the vetting."

**Q: What's the event format?**
A: "7:00–7:20 PM check-in + cocktails. 7:30–9:00 PM structured 6-minute introductions (speed dating adjacent, but social). 9:00–10:00 PM free-form cocktails, mingling, house tour. You can leave whenever. No pressure to stay all night."

**Q: Do I have to participate in the introductions?**
A: "We recommend it. The structured introductions help you meet everyone. But if you prefer to mingled naturally, that works too. It's your evening."

**Q: How selective is the approval process?**
A: "Fairly selective. We aim for a 50–60% approval rate. We look for fit: Are you in the target demographic? Is your guest legitimate? Is your energy right for the community? We're not snobby, but we are selective."

**Q: What if I get rejected?**
A: "We'll give you honest feedback. Maybe your application was incomplete. Maybe the timing isn't right. Maybe our community isn't the fit. We encourage reapplication. The bar does drop slightly as demand builds, so try again in a few months."

**Q: Is this a networking event?**
A: "No. This is a dating event. Some of us meet business connections, sure. But the primary goal is romantic connection. If you're here to network, this isn't the right fit."

**Q: What if the event is awkward?**
A: "Dating is awkward. But it's less awkward with 30 people and structure than alone on an app. Plus, worst case, you meet ambitious, interesting people. Even if there's no romantic spark, you'll expand your network in a genuine way. That's a win."

**Q: How many people attend per event?**
A: "30. Always 30. 15 pairs. Gender-balanced. Capped intentionally. Not 300. Not 100. 30."

**Q: What's the approval timeline?**
A: "24–48 hours. We review applications in batches, so sometimes it's same-day, sometimes 2 days."

**Q: Can I attend multiple events?**
A: "Yes. We recommend it. Most people find their people (romantic or otherwise) after 3–4 events. Repeat attendance is encouraged."

**Q: Do we take photos?**
A: "We may ask permission to take photos for testimonials and marketing. All photos are opt-in. We never share without permission."

**Q: Is this for gay and straight singles?**
A: "Currently, we host mixed-gender (hetero) events. We're planning to add same-gender events soon. Reach out if interested."

**Q: What if I have a specific question not answered here?**
A: "Email us at [email] or reach out in your application. We respond to everything."

**Design Notes:**
- Accordion layout (click to expand)
- 2–3 questions per row on desktop, full width on mobile
- Charcoal text on cream background
- Keep answers to 2–3 sentences when possible
- Group by topic: "About the Event," "Pricing & Logistics," "Application & Approval," "Safety & Community"

## USER FLOWS

### Application Flow
```
User lands on home → "Apply for the next Deuce" button → Scrolls through How It Works, Venues, Testimonials for context → Clicks Apply → Fills form → Submits → Confirmation page → Waits for email approval
```

### Returning User Flow (Repeat Attendee)
```
User lands on home → Maybe reviews testimonials → Clicks Apply → Fills form (shorter path, maybe some info pre-filled) → Submits → Confirmation
```

### Objection Handler Flow
```
User lands on home → Skeptical → Reads How It Works → Still has questions → Goes to FAQ → Gets answers → More confident → Scrolls Testimonials for social proof → Applies
```

## MOBILE RESPONSIVE BEHAVIOR

**Breakpoints:**
- Mobile: 375px–768px
- Tablet: 768px–1024px
- Desktop: 1024px+

**Mobile-Specific Changes:**
- Stack columns to single column
- Reduce font sizes slightly (maintain readability)
- Collapse navigation to hamburger menu
- Make buttons full-width or larger touch targets (44px minimum height)
- Reduce padding/margins slightly to conserve space
- Images full-width with aspect ratio maintained
- Form inputs full-width with large font size (16px minimum to avoid zoom on iOS)

---

## COPY TONE THROUGHOUT

- Conversational, not corporate
- Confident, not arrogant
- Honest about dating awkwardness, but not cheesy
- Emphasize quality, community, intentionality
- Use "we" language (we're building something, we curate the room)
- Avoid urgency language ("limited spots," "last chance")
- Avoid generic dating language ("find your soulmate," "your perfect match")
- Use tennis references sparingly (only in headlines, CTAs, and brand moments)

---

## SUCCESS METRICS FOR DESIGN

When Claude Design creates mockups, they should:
1. ✓ Be clean and minimal (lots of whitespace, not cramped)
2. ✓ Showcase venue photography prominently (Agi House, Portola)
3. ✓ Feature testimonials with VENUE STORY emphasis
4. ✓ Have clear, obvious CTAs ("Apply for the next Deuce")
5. ✓ Use only charcoal, cream, white palette
6. ✓ Feel sophisticated and timeless (not trendy)
7. ✓ Be fully responsive (mobile-first)
8. ✓ Have generous typography (readable, not cramped)
9. ✓ Emphasize bring-a-friend mechanic clearly
10. ✓ Feel like a "community" not a "dating startup"
