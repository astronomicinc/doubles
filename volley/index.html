Why this works

It changes the shape of the night.

Three small mechanics, working together. The Volley isn't decoration — it's how we engineer chemistry in a room of strangers.

01

No awkward intros.

Everyone arrives focused on the speaker. You get to listen and observe, not perform. The pressure to "be on" the moment you walk in quietly disappears.

02

Instant conversation.

You all just heard the same story. Dinner conversation has a shared starting point, so the first ten minutes flow naturally instead of stalling on weather and job titles.

03

Inspiring, always.

Even if you don't exchange numbers, you'll leave with a story worth telling and an idea worth chasing. No Doubles night is a wasted night.

The format

How the night moves.

We orchestrate the first half so the second half can run itself. Doors at six; the fireside lands at 6:30, the room finds its rhythm by 7:30.

  1. 6:00Doors & networking

    Cocktail-party energy.

    Upbeat jazz on the speakers, golden-hour light through a wall of glass, bay views in every direction — and a glass in your hand inside ninety seconds. Thirty minutes to mingle and shake some hands before the room sits at 6:30.

  2. 6:30The fireside

    Fifteen minutes, one story.

    Our speaker, in conversation with one of the founders. The story, the lessons, the candor. You leave knowing something you didn't when you arrived — and the room shares a moment to talk about.

  3. 6:45The Q&A

    Forty-five minutes for the room.

    This is the move. Every question you hear is a thread someone at dinner can pull on. By 7:30, every guest has heard every other guest say something interesting — the icebreaker work is done.

  4. 7:30Dinner party

    An open buffet, dinner-party energy.

    Catered by a kitchen we love and laid out for grazing — shared plates you build yourself, hand-picked wine, the music up just enough. The room moves: half standing, half sitting, knots of conversation forming and dissolving. By nine, you've met half the room.
